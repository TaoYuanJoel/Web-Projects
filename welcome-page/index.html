Reading Response 1: "Critical Atlas of Internet"

Original Website

>>Though a series of 15 hypotheses, this Critical Atlas of Internet aims to develop 15 conceptual spatialization exercises. The purpose of the atlas is to use spatial analysis as a key to understanding social, political and economic issues on Internet. The atlas seek to discern the shape of the internet. The atlas seek to discern the shape of the Internet in order to understand the concrete issues involved.
>>The automatic personalization of the user’s environment has been the dream of numerous designers. The active and even intrusive use of the ‘digital self’ poses problems of freedom of thought and neutrality. There are as many Internet architectures as there are users.

Reading Response 2: "A Vernacular Web"

Original Website

At the moment, there is a retro wave in web design. That is to use the design style used in the era of web design not as efficient, diverse and formatted as today, and the content is given a new meaning.
As one of the first pioneers in web design the author shows us how to do it in the right way. These include specific glyphs, exaggerated pictures, and some specific uses and usages. It also largely allowed me to step out of the familiar web paradigm. I learned about so many strange design methods.
